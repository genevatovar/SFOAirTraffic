SFO Through Crisis:
A Passenger Story

When the world stops moving, airports feel it first.

SFO is one of the most recognized airports in the United States, known for its museum exhibits, dining, and sensory rooms. But behind that reputation is a passenger traffic story shaped by forces far outside the airport's control. This project traces over 25 years of monthly enplaned passenger data, from the aftermath of 9/11 and the 2008 recession to the near-total collapse of air travel during COVID-19 and the slow climb back. Through four visualizations, it explores how deep each disruption cut, whether domestic and international routes recovered at the same pace, which airlines grew or pulled back, and what seasonal patterns look like when you zoom out across two and a half decades.

01 — Overview

Seeing the full picture

Each cell is one month at SFO. The darker the cell, the more passengers flew that month. Summer peaks repeat every year — until COVID-19 nearly wipes out an entire row in 2020.

02 — Recovery

Domestic vs. international

Domestic routes bounced back faster. International traffic lagged significantly — travel bans, closed borders, and reduced demand kept those routes suppressed well into 2022.

03 — Airlines

Who grew, who pulled back

United dominates SFO throughout, but the pandemic reshuffled the carriers below it. Some never returned to their pre-COVID volume, while others used the disruption to grow their share.

04 — Global

Where SFO connects

Asia and Europe anchor SFO's international reach. Toggle between eras to watch those connections shrink during the crisis and slowly rebuild through recovery.
